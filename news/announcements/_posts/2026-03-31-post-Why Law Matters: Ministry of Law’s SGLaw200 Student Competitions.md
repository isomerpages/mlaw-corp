<p><strong><u>About SGLaw200</u></strong>
</p>
<p>The Ministry of Law advances access to justice, the rule of law, the economy
and society through policy, law and services.</p>
<p>This year, we mark SGLaw200 – 200 years since Singapore’s modern judicial
and legal system was established via the Second Charter of Justice in 1826.
Our legal system is the foundation of the Rule of Law, which is a fundamental
principle underpinning Singapore’s development. It has driven our economic
growth, maintained racial and religious harmony, and supported our key
social policies.</p>
<p>SGLaw200 reiterates our commitment to the Rule of Law, and commemorates
the past, present and future of Singapore’s legal system. A trusted legal
system, a trusted Singapore.</p>
<p><strong><u>About Ministry of Law’s SGLaw200 Student Competitions</u></strong>
</p>
<p>As part of the series of events to commemorate SGLaw200, and to reiterate
our commitment to the Rule of Law together, the Ministry of Law has organised
a range of student competitions. We invite Singapore Citizen or Permanent
Resident students to share their appreciation and understanding of the
Rule of Law and its impact on daily life through these competitions. Students
do <u>not</u> need to have prior legal knowledge to participate.</p>
<p>There are three categories of competitions:</p>
<p>a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Upper Primary (Primary 4 to 6);</p>
<p>b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Secondary (Secondary 1 to 5); and</p>
<p>c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Pre-University (Institute of Technical
Education, Junior College, Polytechnic).</p>
<p>Prizes worth up to $250 for individual entries for the Upper Primary and
Secondary categories, and up to $1,000 for group entries for the Pre-University
category, are up for grab!</p>
