<p>The Ministry of Law and many of our partners provide legal assistance
to those who need it:</p>
<p>a. The Legal Aid Bureau (LAB) provides legal advice, legal assistance,
and legal aid on civil matters, to Singapore citizens and Permanent Residents
with limited means. Please visit <a href="https://go.gov.sg/mlawlabesvc" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://go.gov.sg/mlawlabesvc</a> for
more information on the eligibility criteria and to submit an online application.</p>
<p>b. The <a href="https://pdo.mlaw.gov.sg/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Public Defender's Office (PDO)</a> and
<a href="https://www.probono.sg/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Pro Bono SG (PBSG)’s Criminal Legal Aid Scheme (CLAS)</a>co-deliver criminal
defence aid to accused persons with limited means who are charged with
non-capital offences. Please visit PDO's website for information on the
<a href="https://pdo.mlaw.gov.sg/criminal-defence-aid/qualifying-for-help/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">eligibility criteria</a>and <a href="https://pdo.mlaw.gov.sg/criminal-defence-aid/how-to-apply/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">application process</a>.</p>
<p>c. Pro Bono SG’s <a href="https://www.probono.sg/get-legal-help/legal-guidance/the-general-public/legal-clinics-in-singapore" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Community Legal Clinics</a> provide
general legal guidance on personal issues to those who are unable to afford
a lawyer and have not yet obtained legal advice. The <a href="https://www.probono.sg/get-legal-help/legal-guidance/the-general-public/legal-clinics-in-singapore" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">website</a> also
includes information on legal clinics in Singapore run by other organisations.</p>
<p>d. Pro Bono SG’s Community Law Centres bring legal assistance closer to
the heartlands. For more information, please visit <a href="https://www.probono.sg/contact-us" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.probono.sg/contact-us</a>.</p>
<p>2. The public may also continue to access online tools and websites that
provide useful information:</p>
<p>a. Pro Bono SG’s LawGoWhere at <a href="https://www.lawgowhere.sg/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.lawgowhere.sg</a> is a first-stop
portal for individuals facing legal crises to get basic information and
be directed to holistic help.</p>
<p>b. <a href="https://eservices.mlaw.gov.sg/lsra/search-lawyer-or-law-firm/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">The Legal Services Regulatory Authority (LSRA) website</a> maintains
a database of all practising lawyers and all licensed law practices in
Singapore. The advanced search function allows searches by area of practice,
as well as by category of registration or license to practise.</p>
<p>c. <a href="https://www.lawsociety.org.sg/find-a-lawyer-2024" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">The Law Society of Singapore website</a> has
a directory of law practices, sorted based on area of practice. It is not
a complete list of all law practices, only those which have chosen to advertise
on the website.</p>
<p>d. <a href="https://www.asialawnetwork.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">The Asia Law Network (ALN) website</a> has
a directory of practising lawyers in Singapore and Asia, sorted by area
of practice, language and country of practice.</p>
<p>e. <a href="https://singaporelegaladvice.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">The Singapore Legal Advice website</a> has
a directory of practising lawyers and their contact details, sorted by
area of practice.</p>
