23 March 2026 Posted in [Press releases](/news/press-releases)

{:start=“1”}
1.&nbsp;The Minister for Law and Second Minister for Home Affairs Mr Edwin Tong SC has instructed&nbsp; for a Correction Direction, under the Protection from Online Falsehoods and Manipulation Act&nbsp; 2019 (POFMA), to be issued to Xu Yuan Chen (alias Terry Xu) and Miao Yi Infotech Ltd for&nbsp; their publications which communicated false statements of fact concerning the appointment&nbsp; of the Attorney-General:&nbsp;

<p style="margin-left: 40px">(i) An article on The Online Citizen’s (“TOC’s”) website (theonlinecitizen.com) on 9 March&nbsp; 2026 and posted on <a href="[https://www.theonlinecitizen.com](https://www.theonlinecitizen.com)">https://www.theonlinecitizen.com</a> as well as <a href="[https://heidoh.com](https://heidoh.com)">https://heidoh.com;</a></p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px">(ii) TOC’s Facebook post on 9 March 2026; and</p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px">(iii) TOC’s Instagram post on 9 March 2026.</p>

{:start=“2”}&nbsp;
2.&nbsp;Xu Yuan Chen (alias Terry Xu) and Miao Yi Infotech Ltd are required to publish Correction&nbsp; Notices on TOC’s websites, Heidoh’s website, TOC’s Facebook and TOC’s Instagram pages,&nbsp; and in The Straits Times. These notices will state that the content communicated false&nbsp; statements of fact, and provide a link to the Government’s clarification.&nbsp;

{:start=“3”}&nbsp;
3.&nbsp;For the facts of the case, please refer to the Factually article at the following link: <a href="https://www.factually.gov.sg/corrections-and-clarifications/230326">https://www.factually.gov.sg/corrections-and-clarifications/230326</a>&nbsp;
