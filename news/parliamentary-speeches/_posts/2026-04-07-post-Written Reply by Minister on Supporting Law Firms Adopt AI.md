<b><u>Name and Constituency of Member of Parliament</u></b><br>
<b>Ms Hany Soh (Member of Parliament for Marsiling-Yew Tee GRC) </b>

<b><u>Question</u></b><br>
To ask the Minister for Law whether the Ministry will consider establishing grants or subsidies to support local law firms in adopting Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools according to each firm’s needs.

<b><u>Written Answer:</u></b><br>
{:start="1"}
1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; There are already grant programmes in place to support law firms to adopt technology, including Artificial Intelligence (“AI”) tools.

{:start="2"}
2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Since 2022, MinLaw has provided funding support via the Productivity Solutions Grant for the Legal Sector (PSG-Legal) to help law firms to digitalise. PSG-Legal today defrays 50% of firms’ first-year cost of adopting pre-approved legaltech solutions, capped at S$45,000 per year. Supported solution categories include AI tools such as legal chatbots and document review software.

{:start="3"}
3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Law firms can also tap on Enterprise Singapore’s Enterprise Development Grant (EDG) to upgrade and innovate, or the Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG) for non-legaltech solutions to improve their productivity and automate their processes.

{:start="4"}
4.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Enabling AI adoption is not only about addressing cost but also providing support for change management, especially for smaller law firms. We recognise that many lawyers are kept busy by their daily work, and may not have the capacity to dedicate additional hours to researching and implementing solutions.

{:start="5"}
5.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; We therefore launched the pilot of the Legal Innovation and Future-Readiness Transformation (“LIFT”) initiative in June 2025. Under LIFT, MinLaw deploys legaltech consultants to law firms to oversee the end-to-end change process. The legaltech consultants will help law firms diagnose their technology needs, recommend suitable legaltech solutions and implement the chosen solution.
