<p><u>In-person taking of evidence by foreign authorities and agents in Singapore is not permitted.</u> Foreign
court officials, authorities, and their agents (including&nbsp; private
counsel appointed to serve as agents) may not, while physically present
in Singapore, obtain evidence from persons in Singapore for the purpose
of foreign non-criminal proceedings, even where the evidence taken is voluntary.
In that regard, Singapore has entered a reservation to disapply Chapter
II of the <em><a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/specialised-sections/evidence" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Hague Convention on the Taking of Evidence Abroad in Civil or Commercial Matters 1970</a></em> (the
“<strong>Hague Evidence Convention</strong>”). Evidence may instead be
taken through (a) judicial assistance from the Singapore courts, or (b)
remote means with, where required, prior permission from the Singapore
Government.</p>
<p><u>Taking of evidence involving legal compulsion requires judicial assistance</u>.
Where the obtaining of evidence in Singapore for use in foreign non-criminal
proceedings involves legal compulsion, judicial assistance from the Singapore
courts is required. This applies to the obtaining of evidence through in-person
or remote means. This obtaining of evidence includes, among others, the
examination of witnesses (either orally or in writing) or the production
of documents.</p>
<p><u>Remote-taking of voluntary evidence.</u> Where voluntary evidence is
to be obtained, without legal compulsion, from a person in Singapore through
remote means (i<em>.e</em>. via video and/or audio link), for the purpose
of foreign court or tribunal proceedings in non-criminal matters, judicial
assistance from the Singapore courts may not be required. For further information,
please refer to the page “<strong><a href="" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Obtaining Voluntary Evidence Remotely from a Person in Singapore for Foreign Proceedings</a>”</strong>.</p>
<p><strong><u>Seeking Judicial Assistance</u></strong>
</p>
<p>The procedures for seeking judicial assistance to obtain evidence in Singapore
for use in foreign court or tribunal proceedings are governed by the <strong><a href="https://sso.agc.gov.sg/Act/ECPOJA1979" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Evidence (Civil Proceedings in Other Jurisdictions) Act 1979</a></strong> and <strong><a href="https://sso.agc.gov.sg/SL/scja1969-s914-2021?ProvIds=P12-PO55-#P12-PO55-pr2" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Order 55 of the Rules of Court 2021</a></strong>.</p>
<p>There are two principal means of doing so:</p>
<p>(i) <strong><u>Court application</u></strong>: A duly authorised person
may apply, on behalf of the relevant foreign court or tribunal, to the
General Division of the High Court of Singapore, pursuant to Order 55,
rule 2 of the Rules of Court 2021. Parties may wish to engage qualified
Singapore lawyers to make this application.</p>
<p>(ii) <strong><u>Letter of Request</u></strong>: Where no person has been
duly authorised on behalf of the foreign court or tribunal to make the
above application, and where a Civil Procedure Convention is applicable,
a letter of request may be transmitted by the relevant foreign authorities
to the Registrar of the Supreme Court,<strong> </strong>pursuant to Order
55, rule 3(b) of the Rules of Court 2021.</p>
<p>Singapore is a party to the following Civil Procedure Conventions:</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The <em><a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/specialised-sections/evidence" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Hague Convention on the Taking of Evidence Abroad in Civil or Commercial Matters 1970</a></em> (the <strong>“Hague Evidence Convention</strong>”).
The Supreme Court of Singapore serves as Singapore's Central Authority
under the Hague Evidence Convention. For further information on seeking
judicial assistance from Singapore under the Hague Evidence Convention,
please refer to: <a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/states/authorities/details3/?aid=532" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.hcch.net/en/states/authorities/details3/?aid=532</a>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The bilateral Civil Procedure Conventions set out in the <a href="https://sso.agc.gov.sg/SL/scja1969-s914-2021?ProvIds=P12-PO70-Sc3-#P12-PO70-Sc3-" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Third Schedule of the Rules of Court 2021</a>.
Online copies of these bilateral Civil Procedure Conventions can be found
here.</p>
</li>
</ol>
