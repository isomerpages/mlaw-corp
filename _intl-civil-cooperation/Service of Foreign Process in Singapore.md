<p>Singapore is a party to the <em><a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/full-text/?cid=17" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Hague Convention on the Service Abroad of Judicial and Extrajudicial Documents in Civil or Commercial Matters 1965</a> </em>(“<strong>Hague Service Convention</strong>”).
The Hague Service Convention simplifies and streamlines processes for service
of judicial and extrajudicial documents in civil or commercial matters
in jurisdictions that are party to the Convention. The list of parties
to the Convention is available at this <a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/status-table/?cid=17" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">status table</a> on
the website of the Hague Conference on Private International Law. The Ministry
of Law is Singapore’s Central Authority for the Hague Service Convention.</p>
<p>If the document to be served in Singapore is from a jurisdiction that
is a party to the Hague Service Convention, and falls within the scope
of the Hague Service Convention, it must be served through one of the following
channels:<a href="#_ftn1" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><sup>1</sup></a>
</p>
<p>(a) The main transmission channel under Article 5 of the Hague Service
Convention. Information on the procedure and requirements for utilising
this channel are set out <a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/states/authorities/details3/?aid=1200" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">here</a>.</p>
<p>(b) If another Civil Procedure Convention applies,<a href="#_ftn2" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><sup>2</sup></a> through the channels provided under that
convention, if the serving party so elects.<a href="#_ftn3" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><sup>3</sup></a>
</p>
<p>These channels include the sending of a letter of request from a consular
or other authority of the sending jurisdiction to the Registrar of the
Supreme Court of Singapore. The procedure and requirements for utilising
this channel are set out in Order 64 rule 4 of the Rules of Court 2021.</p>
<p>(c) If there are exceptional circumstances, the indirect diplomatic channel
referred to in Article 9, paragraph 2 of the Hague Service Convention.</p>
<p><strong>A.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Service in Singapore where the Hague Service Convention does not apply</u></strong>
</p>
<p>If the document to be served in Singapore is not from a jurisdiction that
is a party to the Hague Service Convention, or falls outside the scope
of the Hague Service Convention, it may be served in Singapore through
the channels set out in the following rules in Order 64 of the Rules of
Court 2021:</p>
<p>(a) Rule 2: Service pursuant to a letter of request from the foreign court
or tribunal to the Ministry of Law;</p>
<p>(b) Rule 3: Alternative modes of service; or</p>
<p>(c) Rule 4: Where a Civil Procedure Convention<a href="#_ftn4" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><sup>4</sup></a> (other than the Hague Service Convention)
applies, service pursuant to a letter of request from a consular or other
authority of the Contracting State to the Registrar of the Supreme Court
of Singapore requesting service on a person in Singapore.</p>
