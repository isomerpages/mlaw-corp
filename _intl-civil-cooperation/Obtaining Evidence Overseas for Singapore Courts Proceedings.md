<p>Witnesses physically located in foreign jurisdictions may give evidence
in Singapore court proceedings through both non-remote and remote means
&nbsp;(i.e. through live video link or live television link). Such evidence-taking
must comply with the requirements under Singapore law (including the Evidence
Act 1893 and the applicable Rules of Court) and any applicable practice
directions issued by the Singapore courts.</p>
<p>Relevant requirements under Singapore law may include:</p>
<p>(a)&nbsp;For the taking of evidence through non-remote means: Order 9
rule 24 of the Rules of Court 2021</p>
<p>(b)&nbsp;For the taking of evidence through remote means: Section 62A
of the Evidence Act 1893; Order 15, rule 5 of the Rules of Court 2021;
paragraph 77 of the Supreme Court Practice Directions 2021; paragraph 59
of the State Courts Practice Directions 2021</p>
<p>The parties seeking evidence must also ensure that such evidence-taking
is permitted by the foreign jurisdiction in which the witness is located.
Depending on the foreign jurisdiction, there may be restrictions, prohibitions
or requirements on witnesses giving evidence for court proceedings in Singapore.
Litigants should comply with all requirements of the relevant foreign jurisdiction
prior to taking evidence for Singapore court proceedings.</p>
<p>In respect of the obtaining of evidence through remote means, information
on the requirements of specified foreign jurisdictions that must be complied
with can be found <a href="https://www.singaporelawwatch.sg/About-Singapore-Law/Civil-Practice-ADR/requirements-of-foreign-jurisdictions-on-the-taking-of-evidence-given-voluntarily-by-way-of-live-video-link-from-a-witness-located-in-their-jurisdiction-in-civil-proceedings-before-s" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">here</a>.</p>
<p>Please note that the Ministry of Law is not involved in outgoing requests
to foreign jurisdictions to obtain evidence.</p>
