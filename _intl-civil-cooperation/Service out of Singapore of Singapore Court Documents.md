<p><strong>A.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Service out of Singapore where the Hague Service Convention applies</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Singapore is a party to the <em><a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/full-text/?cid=17" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Hague Convention on the Service Abroad of Judicial and Extrajudicial Documents in Civil or Commercial Matters 1965</a> </em>(“<strong>Hague Service Convention</strong>”).
The Hague Service Convention simplifies and streamlines processes for service
of judicial and extrajudicial documents in civil or commercial matters
in jurisdictions that are party to the Convention. The list of parties
to the Convention is available at this <a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/status-table/?cid=17" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">status table</a> on
the website of the Hague Conference on Private International Law.</p>
<p>If the Singapore court document is to be served in a jurisdiction that
is a party to the Hague Service Convention, and falls within the scope
of the Hague Service Convention, it may be served through one of the following
channels:</p>
<p>(a) The main transmission channel under Article 5 of the Hague Service
Convention. The procedure and requirements for utilising this channel are
set out in Order 8, Rule 4A of the Rules of Court 2021. Information on
any costs payable to effect service in the relevant country may be found
on that country’s <a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/authorities1/?cid=82" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Central Authority and Practical Information page</a> on
the website of the Hague Conference on Private International Law.</p>
<p>(b) To the extent permitted by the Hague Service Convention and not objected
to by the receiving Contracting State, any of the applicable channels for
service out of Singapore, as set out in Order 8, Rule 2 of the Rules of
Court 2021 or the equivalent provisions in the Singapore International
Commercial Court Rules 2021 and Family Justice Rules 2014. The objections
made by Contracting States to transmission channels for service abroad
may be found at this <a href="https://www.hcch.net/en/instruments/conventions/status-table/?cid=17" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">status table</a> on
the website of the Hague Conference on Private International Law.</p>
<p>(c) If another Civil Procedure Convention applies, through the channel
provided under that Convention, if the serving party so elects. The list
of Civil Procedure Conventions that Singapore is a party to is set out
in the Third Schedule of the Rules of Court 2021.</p>
<p><strong>B.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Service out of Singapore where the Hague Service Convention does not apply</u></strong>
</p>
<p>If the Singapore court document is to be served in a jurisdiction that
is not a party to the Hague Service Convention, or falls outside the scope
of the Hague Service Convention, service out may be effected through an
applicable channel set out in Order 8, Rules 2 to 4 of the Rules of Court
2021 or the equivalent provisions in the Singapore International Commercial
Court Rules 2021 and Family Justice Rules 2014.</p>
<p>Please note that the Ministry of Law is not involved in outgoing requests
to serve Singapore court documents overseas.</p>
