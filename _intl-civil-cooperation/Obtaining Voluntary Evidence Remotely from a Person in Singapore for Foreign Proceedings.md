<p>This provides information on how to obtain <u>voluntary</u> evidence remotely
(i.e. via video and/or audio link) from a person physically in Singapore
for the purpose of foreign court or tribunal proceedings in non-criminal
matters.</p>
<p>The procedure for doing so depends on whether the foreign jurisdiction
for which the evidence is to be obtained is a listed or non-listed jurisdiction.
Jurisdictions are listed where they have been ascertained by the Singapore
Government to accord Singapore substantial reciprocity.</p>

<p>The Ministry of Law is working on further arrangements to add additional
jurisdictions to the above list.</p>
<p>Please note that Singapore does not consider Chapter I of the Hague Evidence
Convention to apply to the remote taking of voluntary evidence. Such remote
taking of evidence would be done directly by the foreign court or tribunal
(or their authorised agents), and not by the requested State. Singapore
has entered a reservation to disapply Chapter II of the Hague Evidence
Convention.</p>
<p>If evidence is to be obtained under legal compulsion (i.e. it is not voluntary),
judicial assistance from the Singapore courts must be sought and the procedure
set out in this section would not apply. Please see <a href="" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">Obtaining Evidence in Singapore for Foreign Proceedings</a> for
more information.</p>
<p><strong>A.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Listed jurisdictions</u></strong>
</p>
<p>For listed jurisdictions, Singapore will not require prior permission
to be sought from the Singapore Government for a foreign court or tribunal
to take voluntary evidence remotely from witnesses in Singapore. This is
subject to the following conditions:</p>
<p>(a) the witness is not giving evidence from Singapore government or court
premises;</p>
<p>(b) the witness is giving evidence voluntarily and is not under compulsion
to do so; and</p>
<p>(c) the foreign court or tribunal proceedings in question –</p>
<p>(i) are not criminal proceedings;</p>
<p>(ii) are not (1) proceedings where a direct outcome contemplated is the
imposition of public penalties or other sanctions, or the imposition or
recovery of taxes; and are not (2) proceedings that are directly linked
to such proceedings; and</p>
<p>(iii)&nbsp; do not involve a State, a government, a governmental agency,
or any person acting for a State, a government or a governmental agency,
as a party to the proceedings.</p>
<p>Where the above conditions are met, the said foreign court or tribunal,
or the party seeking the voluntary evidence of the witness in Singapore,
is only required <u>to notify the Singapore Government prior to such remote taking of voluntary evidence</u>,
using the online form at the following link: [<strong>link</strong>].</p>
<p>Where a jurisdiction is listed and the witness is giving evidence voluntarily,
but the proceedings in question are excluded by the conditions in (c)(ii)
and (iii) above, the Singapore Government’s prior permission is to be sought,
pursuant to the procedure at sub-section C below.</p>
<p><strong>B.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Non-listed jurisdictions</u></strong>
</p>
<p>For jurisdictions that are not listed, Singapore will require prior permission
to be sought from the Singapore Government in order for a foreign court
or tribunal to take voluntary evidence remotely from witnesses in Singapore.</p>
<p><strong>C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Where Singapore’s prior permission is required</u></strong>
</p>
<p>For voluntary evidence to be taken remotely in Singapore in non-criminal
matters, the Singapore Government’s prior permission is required where:</p>
<p>(a) the jurisdiction seeking to take the evidence is a non-listed jurisdiction;
or</p>
<p>(b) the jurisdiction is listed, but the proceeding:</p>
<p>(i) is (1) one where a direct outcome contemplated is the imposition of
public penalties or other sanctions, or the imposition or recovery of taxes;
or (2) directly linked to such proceedings; or</p>
<p>(ii) involves a State, a government, a governmental agency, or any person
acting for a State, a government or a governmental agency, as a party to
the proceedings.</p>
<p>In cases where prior permission is required, a formal request for prior
permission should be made by the relevant authorities of the jurisdiction
to the <strong>Ministry of Law of the Republic of Singapore</strong> via
the following link: [<strong>link</strong>]. Requests made by persons other
than the relevant authorities of the jurisdiction will not be accepted.
These requests should contain the following information:</p>
<p>(a) the case name and identifying number (if any);</p>
<p>(b)&nbsp;a description of the proceedings;</p>
<p>(c)&nbsp;regarding the witness in Singapore to whom the request relates:</p>
<p>(i) the full name, nationality and address of the witness;</p>
<p>(ii) the relevance of the witness to the case, including whether he or
she is a witness of fact or an expert witness;</p>
<p>(iii) whether the witness is represented by counsel, and if so, the name
and address of the counsel;</p>
<p>(iv) the reason(s) why the witness is providing evidence remotely, from
Singapore; &nbsp;</p>
<p>(v) confirmation that the witness is giving evidence voluntarily; and</p>
<p>(vi) confirmation that the witness shall not be subject to any penalty
or liability or otherwise prejudiced in law by reason only of the witness’s
refusal or failure to consent to give evidence remotely as requested.</p>
<p>The Singapore Government will assess such requests on a case-by-case basis,
with processing times subject to the volume of requests received.</p>
